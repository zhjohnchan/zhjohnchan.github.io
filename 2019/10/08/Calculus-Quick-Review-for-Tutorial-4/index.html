[Calculus] Quick Review for Tutorial 4

Zhihong Chen
2019-10-08

1. Definition of continuity
Let $f: D \rightarrow R$ be a function, where $D$ is an interval and let $c\in D$. Then f is said to be continuous at $c$ if for every $\epsilon > 0$, there exists a $\delta > 0$ such that,

$$|f(x)-f(c)|<\epsilon,\ for\ all\ x \in D\ satisfying\ |x-c|<\delta.$$

2. The theorem of limits of continuous functions
if $f(x)$ is continuous at a point b and $lim_{x\rightarrow c}g(x)=b$,
$$lim_{x\rightarrow c}f(g(x))=f(lim_{x\rightarrow c}g(x))=f(b).$$

3. The kinds of discontinuous

1) Removable discontinuities
2) Jumping discontinuities
3) Essential discontinuities

4. A Computational Technique
if $lim_{x\rightarrow c} {f(x) \over g(x)} = 1$, we can replace $f(x)$ with $g(x)$ when computing the limit of $f(x)h(x)$.
